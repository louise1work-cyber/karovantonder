About

Born of wind & water.

Karo was twelve years old when her father first introduced her to windsurfing during their annual family December holiday on the West Coast in Langebaan. Two decades later she is one of the fastest women on the planet - a multi-discipline professional rider who calls Langebaan, South Africa, home.

On the water she races slalom, hunts down speed records and rides waves - pushing her limits in the 40-knot storms that other riders run from.

Disciplines

Three boards. One rider.

Few windsurfers compete seriously across multiple disciplines. Karo does - from flat-water speed runs at over 80 km/h, to head-to-head slalom, to riding the surf back home in Langebaan.

Speed

500m runs in the gnarliest winds on earth. Personal best of 46 knots over 500 m - with a top speed of 48 knots (89 km/h). 5th fastest woman of all time.

Slalom

High-stakes head-to-head racing through downwind courses. IFCA African Slalom Champion and a fixture on the international scene.

Wave

Back home in Langebaan and across the Cape coastline, Karo trains in real ocean conditions on a 73L Severne wave board.
